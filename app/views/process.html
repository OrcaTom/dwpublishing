The publishing process

Once your request is received by Keith or Tom you'll get a response. The response may:

  • ask you for more information about the change
  • agree your request
  • reject your request, and give you a reason

If you don't agree with a rejection then you can discuss with Keith or Tom directly in the first instance.

If your request is agreed

A ticket will be raised with the relevant team (either GDS or DWP digital publishing, depending on the type of content) and put in their backlog.

If the request involves creating a completely new page or section on GOV.UK you may have to join a call for further discussion. This may include colleagues from GDS.

Once a ticket is raised

The work will be prioritised against all the requests in the team's current backlog.

GDS prioritise their work against requests from all government departments, not just DWP.

GDS or the publishing team will draft the content to meet the GOV.UK style guide and send the draft page to check for factual accuracy.

Fact checking

You'll be sent a link to a draft of the content.

You should review the content and respond with either:

  • confirmation the content is factually accurate
  • comments on where the content is inaccurate and why

This isn't an opportunity to 'sign-off' or suggest changes to the content. The content will have been designed to the GDS style guide.

If factual corrections are required, changes will be made and you may get another fact check request.

Once a positive fact check response is received the content will be published and you'll be told.
