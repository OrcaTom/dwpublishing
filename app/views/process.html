The publishing process

Once your request is received by Keith or Tom you'll get a response. The response may:

  • ask you for more information, either by email or in a face to face meeting
  • agree your request
  • reject your request, and give you a reason

If you don't agree with a rejection then contact Keith or Tom directly, or if you want to escalate it, Ben Holliday, head of user experience.

If your request is agreed

A request ticket will be raised with GDS.

GDS will put the ticket into their backlog. If the request isn't complicated (eg a factual change to an existing page) it will be actioned within 7 days.

If the request involves creating a new page or content on GOV.UK you may be asked to join a call for further discussion. The work will then be prioritised by GDS against requests from the whole of government. The process may take several weeks to complete.

GDS will draft the content to meet the GOV.UK style guide and send the draft page to check for factual accuracy.

You'll be sent a link to a draft of the content by Keith or Tom.

You should review the content and respond to Keith or Tom with either:

  • confirmation the content is factually accurate
  • comments on where the content is inaccurate and why

You shouldn't comment on the words used or presentation according to your personal reference. All content will be written to meet the style guide.

If corrections are required, changes will be made by GDS and another fact check request will be sent.

Once GDS has a positive fact check response they will publish the change to GOV.UK.
