Who writes content for GOV.UK

The Government Digital Service (GDS) is solely responsible for writing any digital content on GOV.UK intended for citizens or small businesses.

The DWP digital publishing team is responsible for publishing and presenting departmental information on GOV.UK. This includes:

  • corporate reports
  • transparency releases
  • content about the development and implementation of DWP policies
  • guidance for specialist audiences
  • press releases, news stories and speeches
  • statistics
  • research reports
  • printed forms
  • Freedom of Information responses
  • promotional material

The publishing team uses analytics, feedback and user insights from research to design this content.

The publishing teams decide where and how these items appear on GOV.UK, and in what format.

All content must meet:

  • the proposition of what should and shouldn't go on GOV.UK
  • the design principles and style guide
